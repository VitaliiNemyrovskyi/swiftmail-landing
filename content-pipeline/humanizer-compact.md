# Humanizer rules — runtime

Used alongside voice-compact.md. These are the moves AI rarely makes
unprompted. Force ≥3 of them per article.

## 1. Mid-thought pivot
AI is monotonously right. Humans change their minds mid-paragraph.
✅ "Most guides recommend daily emails. Skip that — outdated since iOS 15."
✅ "I assumed bounce-rate spikes meant a list-quality problem. Three days
   of digging — it was DNS."

## 2. Front-load specifics
AI: principle → example. Humans: example → principle.
❌ "Many businesses see significant improvements when they..."
✅ "Bonobos abandoned 11K carts last Q4. Their flow missed three things:"

## 3. Show your work (first-person experience)
✅ "I queried sessions where dwell_time > 90s AND no_purchase. 412 hits last week."
✅ "We migrated [Customer] last Tuesday. 47 minutes end-to-end."
✅ "Our SwiftMail data shows 34% of abandonment is price-related."

## 4. Time / date anchors
AI is timeless. Humans live in time.
✅ "Before iOS 15..."
✅ "After Apple MPP rollout (Sept 2021)..."
✅ "Last Tuesday I watched bounce rate climb from 1.2% to 8.7% in 4 hours."

## 5. Sentence rhythm — VARY
- ≥1 in 5 sentences under 8 words.
- ≥1 in 10 over 25 words.
- 1–2 fragments. Like this.
- Mix paragraph lengths. Some 1-line. Some 5-line.

## 6. Embraced contradictions
AI prefers clean assertions. Humans hold nuance.
✅ "It works. Mostly. The 20% where it doesn't is interesting:"
✅ "Counter-intuitive: more frequency *raised* engagement here. We didn't expect that."

## 7. Contrarian devices (use 1 per article max — pick one)
- Calling someone wrong: "Most SaaS blogs recommend X. They're wrong."
- Self-deprecation: "We tried Y first. It failed. Here's what we learned."
- Sentence-starts-with-number: "11 wasn't enough. We needed 14."

## 8. Start with And / But / Because
Yes, you can. Vary transitions instead of "Furthermore" / "Moreover".

## 9. Imperfect parallelism
AI loves perfect parallel lists. Break the pattern.
❌ "Increase open rates. Reduce bounce rates. Improve deliverability."
✅ "Open rates climbed 8%. Bounce rate finally dropped — took 6 weeks.
   Deliverability is its own story; we'll get there."

## 10. Bullets sparingly (max 1 list per 600 words)
3-item lists → convert to prose:
❌ - Set up SPF / - Configure DKIM / - Enable DMARC
✅ "Set up SPF first, then DKIM, then DMARC last."

## 11. Sensory verbs even in B2B
"Email queue backs up", "dashboard goes red", "bounce rate creeping up".
