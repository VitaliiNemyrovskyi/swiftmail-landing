THE PROBLEM

Your visitors don't ghost you randomly.
They leave for reasons. You just can't see them.

Most marketing tools show you what visitors did — page views, clicks, conversions. None of them tell you why a visitor stalled on the pricing page, why they bailed mid-form, or why one cohort returns three times before buying. Without the why, every recovery campaign is a guess.

FEATURE · 01

Capture every click.
Even the angry ones.

Twelve behavioral signals out of the box — page views, clicks, scroll depth, form-field focus, form abandonment, rage clicks, dead clicks, runtime errors, fetch failures, time on page, exit intent, conversions. One snippet, ~4.4KB gzipped, async-loaded, mask-by-default for privacy. Drop it on your site. Done. No tag manager, no developer ticket, no consent dance.

A visitor rage-clicks a broken discount field. SwiftMail captures the signal in real time.

FEATURE · 02

Don't just see what happened.
Understand why.

Every session gets analyzed by AI that reads the full event timeline and produces a structured reason. Abandoned sessions resolve to one of seven reasons: PRICE_HESITATION, FORM_FRICTION, TRUST_CONCERN, COMPARISON_SHOPPING, DISTRACTION, TECHNICAL_FAILURE, or UNKNOWN. Converted ones resolve to one of six. Each reason ships with a narrative paragraph and a suggested action.

AI reads the timeline, resolves to PRICE_HESITATION (87% confidence), and suggests a recovery action.

FEATURE · 03

One signal.
Eight channels.

From any behavior, fire any channel. Email. SMS. Web push. Mobile push. On-site popup. Retargeting audience. CRM task. Webhook. Same Flow Builder, same trigger system, eight different ways to reach the visitor in the moment that matters. No middleware, no glue code, no integrations spaghetti.

A rage-click signal hits the Flow Builder, fans out to four channels in under a second.

FEATURE · 04

Stitch every session, every channel, every touchpoint into one continuous journey per customer. Five attribution models built in — first-touch, last-touch, linear, position-based, time-decay. Switch any time to see who actually drove the conversion vs. who took the credit. Cross-device merge handles the anonymous-to-known transition without losing acquisition source.

Toggle between five attribution models. Watch credit redistribute across touchpoints in real time.
