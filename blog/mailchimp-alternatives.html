9 Best Mailchimp Alternatives in 2026 (Free & Paid)

April 7, 2026 • 12 min read • SwiftMail Team

Mailchimp used to be the default choice for small-business email marketing. A generous free plan, a friendly interface, and solid deliverability made it the obvious pick for years. But since the Intuit acquisition, the Mailchimp that small businesses loved no longer exists.

If your costs have gone up while your results have gone down, you're not alone. Here are the 9 best Mailchimp alternatives in 2026 — with honest pricing, pros, cons, and a migration checklist at the end.

Why People Are Leaving Mailchimp in 2026

The exodus didn't happen overnight. It's the result of a slow squeeze that has accelerated since 2024:

  • The free plan shrank dramatically — from 2,000 contacts and 10,000 sends/month down to just 500 contacts and 500 sends/month. For most businesses, the free plan is now useless.
  • Pricing rose across every tier — the Standard plan increased by over 40% since 2023, and overage fees can catch you off guard.
  • You pay for contacts you can't email — unsubscribed and archived contacts still count toward your billing total. Many users only discover this after their invoice arrives.
  • Deliverability has slipped — EmailTooltester's 2025 deliverability report gave Mailchimp its lowest rating, with inbox placement dropping below the industry average.
  • Feature bloat — Mailchimp now tries to be a CRM, a website builder, and an ad platform. The email experience has suffered as a result.

"I was paying for people I couldn't even email. 2,000 unsubscribed contacts were silently inflating my bill every month." — Small business owner on Reddit, 2025

What to Look For in a Mailchimp Alternative

Before jumping to another platform, make sure you're evaluating what actually matters:

  • Pricing transparency — Does the platform charge for unsubscribed contacts? Are there overage fees? Is pricing per-contact or per-email?
  • Deliverability — What's their inbox placement rate? Do they handle SPF, DKIM, and DMARC setup? Do they offer a dedicated IP option?
  • Ease of use — Can you set up a campaign in minutes, or do you need a week of tutorials?
  • Automation — Welcome sequences, abandoned cart flows, re-engagement campaigns. How sophisticated can you get without hiring a consultant?
  • Migration support — Can you import your Mailchimp lists, tags, and templates easily? Is there guided migration or do you figure it out alone?

2. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)

Brevo's unique angle is per-email pricing instead of per-contact. You can store unlimited contacts and pay only for the emails you actually send.

  • Pros: Unlimited contacts on all plans, per-email pricing model, built-in SMS and WhatsApp, strong GDPR tools (EU-based company), free plan with 300 emails/day.
  • Cons: Deliverability has been inconsistent (shared IPs on lower tiers), email builder feels dated, advanced automation only on Business plan ($65+/mo).
  • Best for: Budget-conscious businesses and EU-based companies that need GDPR compliance out of the box.
  • Pricing: Free (300 emails/day) → Starter $9/mo (5K emails) → Business $18/mo (5K emails + advanced features). 10K contacts plan: ~$29/mo.

3. MailerLite

MailerLite is the platform people recommend when someone says "I just want something simple and cheap." And for basic email marketing, it delivers.

  • Pros: Genuinely easy to use, generous free plan (1,000 subscribers, 12,000 emails/mo), clean drag-and-drop editor, landing pages and pop-ups included, solid deliverability on higher tiers.
  • Cons: Limited automation (no branching logic on free/basic), approval process can be slow for new accounts, no advanced segmentation or CRM features.
  • Best for: Beginners, bloggers, and small businesses with straightforward email needs.
  • Pricing: Free (1K subs) → Growing Business $15/mo (500 subs) → $54/mo (10K subs). Advanced plan adds automation for $30/mo+.

4. Klaviyo

Klaviyo is the 800-pound gorilla of ecommerce email. If you run a Shopify store and want revenue-attributed automations, Klaviyo is the industry standard.

  • Pros: Deep Shopify/WooCommerce integration, predictive analytics and AI product recommendations, powerful automation flows (browse abandonment, win-back, post-purchase), revenue attribution on every campaign.
  • Cons: Expensive — costs escalate quickly as your list grows, "active profiles" pricing can cause bill shock (similar to Mailchimp's unsubscribed-contact problem), steep learning curve for non-technical users.
  • Best for: Shopify and ecommerce stores doing $500K+/year in revenue.
  • Pricing: Free (250 contacts) → Email $45/mo (1,500 contacts) → ~$100/mo (5K) → $185/mo (10K). SMS add-on is extra.

5. ActiveCampaign

ActiveCampaign is the automation powerhouse. If you need complex, multi-step workflows with conditional logic, scoring, and CRM integration, this is where to look.

  • Pros: Best-in-class automation builder, built-in CRM with deal tracking, advanced segmentation and lead scoring, 950+ integrations, excellent deliverability track record.
  • Cons: No free plan, charges for unsubscribed contacts (like Mailchimp), can feel overwhelming for simple use cases, pricing jumps significantly at higher tiers.
  • Best for: B2B companies, agencies, and anyone who needs sophisticated automation workflows.
  • Pricing: Starter $19/mo (1,000 contacts) → Plus $79/mo (1K contacts + CRM) → $179/mo (10K contacts). No free tier.

6. Kit (formerly ConvertKit)

Kit was built by creators, for creators. If you're a YouTuber, podcaster, author, or course creator, Kit understands your world better than anyone else.

  • Pros: Creator-focused features (paid newsletters, digital product sales, tip jars), clean plain-text email style that feels personal, excellent tagging and segmentation, free plan for up to 10,000 subscribers.
  • Cons: Limited email design options (intentionally minimal), not suited for ecommerce or product businesses, visual automation builder is basic compared to ActiveCampaign, reporting is light.
  • Best for: Content creators, newsletter writers, course sellers, and anyone building an audience.
  • Pricing: Newsletter (free, up to 10K subs) → Creator $29/mo (1K subs) → $99/mo (10K subs). Creator Pro adds advanced features.

7. Beehiiv

Beehiiv exploded in popularity among the newsletter crowd. It's laser-focused on one thing: helping you grow and monetize a newsletter.

  • Pros: Built-in monetization (ad network, paid subscriptions, boosts), referral program tools, SEO-optimized web hosting for archives, generous free plan (2,500 subscribers).
  • Cons: Only for newsletters — no ecommerce flows, no transactional emails, no landing pages, limited automation (just sequences), no traditional campaign-style email marketing.
  • Best for: Newsletter operators who want to monetize their audience.
  • Pricing: Launch (free, 2.5K subs) → Grow $49/mo (10K subs) → Scale $84/mo (10K subs + full features). Enterprise available.

8. Constant Contact

Constant Contact has been around since 1995 and still has a loyal following among small business owners who value phone support and hand-holding.

  • Pros: Extremely easy to use, live phone support (rare in this space), event marketing tools, social media integration, high-quality onboarding resources.
  • Cons: Outdated interface and email editor, expensive for what you get, limited automation on lower tiers, deliverability is middle-of-the-pack, no free plan (only 14-day trial).
  • Best for: Non-technical small business owners who value phone support over features.
  • Pricing: Lite $12/mo (500 contacts) → Standard $35/mo (500 contacts) → ~$110/mo (10K contacts). Overage charges apply.

9. Shopify Email

If you already run a Shopify store and your email needs are basic, Shopify Email is free and built right into your admin panel.

  • Pros: Free for up to 10,000 emails/month, native Shopify integration (products, discounts, order data), no extra app to manage, simple drag-and-drop editor.
  • Cons: Very basic — no automation beyond simple flows, limited segmentation, no A/B testing, not a full-featured email platform, only works with Shopify.
  • Best for: Shopify store owners with basic email needs who want zero additional cost.
  • Pricing: Free (up to 10K emails/mo) → $1 per 1,000 additional emails. No contact-based pricing.

How to Migrate from Mailchimp

Switching email platforms sounds scary, but it's straightforward if you follow these steps:

Step 1: Export Your Data from Mailchimp

  1. Go to Audience → All contacts
  2. Click Export Audience and download the CSV
  3. Export your templates from Campaigns → Email templates (optional)
  4. Note your tags, segments, and automation workflows

Step 2: Import to Your New Platform

  1. Upload your CSV file to the new platform
  2. Map fields (email, first name, last name, tags)
  3. Recreate your key segments
  4. Rebuild your most important automations (welcome sequence, abandoned cart)

Step 3: Update Your DNS Records

  1. Add the new platform's SPF, DKIM, and DMARC records to your domain
  2. Remove Mailchimp's DNS records to avoid conflicts
  3. Verify the new records are propagated (can take up to 48 hours)

Step 4: Test and Switch

  1. Send test campaigns to yourself and your team
  2. Check spam scores and inbox placement
  3. Update any signup forms and embedded widgets on your website
  4. Cancel your Mailchimp plan only after confirming everything works
